## How to choose

- Use semantic tokens for UI, not raw palette values. Primitives (gray-*, blue-*, etc.) are the underlying scale; semantic tokens express intent and should be referenced in screens.
- Main brand CTA / primary action: use `primary-regular`. Step up to `primary-strong` then `primary-heavy` for stronger emphasis (e.g. pressed/active states).
- Feedback and states: use `status-*`. Pick the meaning — `information`, `positive` (success), `caution` (warning), `negative` (error) — then the emphasis level: `weak` for backgrounds/fills, `regular` for the default, `strong` for the strongest emphasis.
- Text hierarchy: use `text-*` in descending prominence — `text-strong` > `text-primary` > `text-secondary` > `text-tertiary`, and `text-disabled` for disabled text.
- Surfaces and separators: use `background-regular` for surfaces, `border-*` for borders, and `divider-*` for separator lines (`weak` vs `regular` for emphasis).
- Decorative or categorical color: use `accent-*` when you need a distinct hue that is not a status or brand action.
- Service contexts: `service-socar` and `service-business` for service-specific branding.
- Location pins: `location-rental` and `location-return`.
- Notifications/badges: `notification-red`.
